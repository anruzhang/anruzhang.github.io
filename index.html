Background

I am the tenured Eugene Anson Stead, Jr. M.D. Associate Professor at the Departments of Biostatistics & Bioinformatics, Computer Science, Electrical & Computer Engineering, Mathematics, and Statistical Science at Duke University. I am a affiliated member at Duke CBB Program. I was an assistant professor of statistics at UW-Madison (2015-2021) affiliated to Machine Learning @ UW-Madison and Institute for Foundations of Data Science (IFDS). I received my Ph.D. from the University of Pennsylvania in 2015 (advised by T. Tony Cai) and my Bachelor's degree in mathematics from Peking University in 2010.

I received the IMS Tweedie Award (2022) (IMS News), ASA Gottfried E. Noether Junior Award (2021), Bernoulli Society New Researcher Award (2021), ICSA Outstanding Young Researcher Award (2021), NSF CAREER Award (2020). I serve as an Associate Editor for Journal of the American Statistical Association (T&M) (starting in 01/01/2023) and a Guest Editor (starting in 10/2021) and an Associate Editor (starting in 07/2022) for Statistics and Its Interface. I serve as an area chair for 2023 International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR).

(Upated in 2022 Fall) For prospective Ph.D. students: Please apply to the Ph.D. programs in Biostatistics & Bioinformatics, Computer Science, and CBB Program. Prospective Ph.D. students interested to work with me can mention this on their applications. Applying to multiple programs at Duke increases your chance and is allowed.

Some key words of my research: tensor learning, high-dimensional statistical inference, statistical learning theory, non-convex optimization, applications in electronic health records, microbiome studies, ... You can find a list of our group's publications from the tab above, on arXiv, or on Google Scholar.

Our group is actively looking for motivated members to work on (1) methodology and theory in machine learning and statistics and/or (2) applications in AI Health.

A postdoc position is available. Joint supervision with other faculty with interests on the foundation of machine learning at Duke CS, ECE, Fuqua, Math, Statistical Science are possible based on your background/interests and further discussions.
